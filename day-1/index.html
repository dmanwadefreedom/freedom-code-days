Daily Read
What Is a Pattern? 5 min

You didn't choose your pattern. It chose you.

Between the ages of 0 and 7, your brain was in theta state — pure programming mode. Every fight your parents had. Every time you were ignored. Every moment you were shamed, praised, or left alone. It all wrote code on your hard drive.

That code is still running.

Your nervous system learned to survive — not thrive. It built a pattern. A strategy. A way of moving through the world that kept you safe when you were small and powerless.

But you're not small anymore. And the survival strategy that saved your life at 5 is destroying your life at 35.

There are 4 core survival patterns:

The Controller — grips everything tight. Can't delegate. Can't trust. Believes if they let go, everything falls apart.

The Runner — leaves before they can be left. New city, new job, new relationship. Motion disguised as progress.

The Ghost — disappears. Goes quiet. Withdraws into their head. Convinces themselves they don't need anyone.

The Chameleon — becomes whoever the room needs them to be. Lost themselves so long ago they don't know who they actually are.

"Until you make the unconscious conscious, it will direct your life and you will call it fate." — Carl Jung

Today we name the pattern. Tomorrow we start dismantling it.

Evening Reflection
Before You Sleep 5 min

No screens after this. Grab your journal. Answer these 3 questions honestly.
