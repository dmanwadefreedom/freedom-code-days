Daily Read
Making Amends Isn't Apologizing 5 min

An apology says "I'm sorry." Amends says "Here's what I did, here's how it affected you, and here's what I'm doing differently."

Amends is action. Amends is changed behavior.

Some amends are direct (conversation). Some are living amends (being different from now on).

You don't owe everyone an apology. But you owe yourself the freedom that comes from cleaning your side of the street.

"The best apology is changed behavior." — Unknown

Daily Action
Make One Amends varies

Your Assignment

Make ONE amends today. Call, text, or write someone you've wronged. If you can't reach them, write the amends letter and read it out loud.

  • Choose one person to make amends to
  • Make the amends (call, text, letter, or living amends)
  • Write about how it felt
  • Read your Day 1 journal entry — see how far you've come

Evening Reflection
Before You Sleep 5 min

No screens after this. Grab your journal. Answer these 3 questions honestly.

  1. How did making amends feel?
  2. What weight lifted (or didn't)?
  3. 21 days in. Read your Day 1 journal entry. How far have you come?

Write it down. Don't just think about it. Ink on paper changes the brain differently than thoughts in your head.
