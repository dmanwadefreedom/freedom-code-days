Daily Read
The Cost of Unforgiveness 5 min

Holding resentment keeps your nervous system in fight-or-flight 24/7.

Your body doesn't know the difference between a real threat and a memory. Every time you replay that scene — the betrayal, the abuse, the abandonment — your cortisol spikes like it's happening right now.

Forgiveness isn't about them. It's about taking your nervous system off a war footing.

"Forgiveness is not an occasional act, it is a constant attitude." — Martin Luther King Jr.

Daily Action
Forgiveness Letter 30 min

Your Assignment

Write a forgiveness letter. To someone who hurt you. To yourself. You don't have to send it. Just write it. Then read it out loud and burn it, delete it, or keep it.

  • Write a forgiveness letter (to someone or yourself)
  • Read it out loud
  • Choose: burn it, delete it, or keep it
  • Write one sentence about how you feel now
Today's Session
Yoga Couple Day 10 — Forgiveness and Letting Go 30 min

Today's session guides you through the practice of releasing what no longer serves you.

Evening Reflection
Before You Sleep 5 min

No screens after this. Grab your journal. Answer these 3 questions honestly.

  1. Who am I still holding resentment toward?
  2. What would my life look like if I let it go?
  3. Can I forgive myself for the ways I've hurt others?

Write it down. Don't just think about it. Ink on paper changes the brain differently than thoughts in your head.
